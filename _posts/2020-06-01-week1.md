

Subject : Modeling Bike Trajectories in Urban Settings Using Cellular Automata Models

Topic : Utilizing Cellular Automata Models to Predict Bike Movements to Optimize Wireless Access Point Deployment in Urban Grids

Research Problem : Ensuring reliable wireless connectivity for bike riders in urban environments is challenging due to the dynamic nature of bike movements and the presence of connectivity dead spots. Cellular automata models can be employed to predict bike trajectories, which can then be used to optimize the deployment of scare wireless access points (WAPs). The challenge lies in effectively modeling these trajectories so WAPs can be strategically placed, ensuring comprehensive coverage and connectivity for all bike riders.


Research Question : "How can Cellular Automata models be used to model the trajectory of bikes across an urban setting to optimize the deployment of wireless access points in order to ensure reliable connectivity for all bike riders?”
