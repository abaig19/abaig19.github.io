Random Waypoint (RWP) mobility model	
- Instead of choosing a completely random endpoint, nodes (representing bikes) could follow predefined routes or paths within the simulation area. These routes could mimic bike lanes or popular cycling routes.
- Nodes could avoid congested areas or choose alternate paths when encountering high-density regions, similar to how cyclists might navigate around heavy traffic areas or crowded bike lanes.

  
Random Direction (RD) Mobility Model	
-  variability in speeds to simulate different biking conditions (slower speeds uphill, faster speeds downhill).
- Nodes may pause at intersections, traffic lights,  mimicking real-world biking behaviors.

  
Gauss-Markov (GM) Mobility Model	
-  Incorporate terrain data (elevation, slope) into the model to influence speed variations, where bikes slow down on uphill slopes and speed up on downhill sections.


Distributed Pheromone Repel (DPR) Mobility Model 	
- bikes could be programmed to favor routes or areas with higher access point density or stronger signal strength to maintain reliable connectivity.
